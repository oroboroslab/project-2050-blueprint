## IV. INFRASTRUCTURE SPECIFICATIONS

### A. Ocean Platform Cities
- **Location:** International waters off continental coasts
- **Status:** Shared global asset — not owned by any single nation
- **Power:** Wave, solar, wind, and substrate harvesting
- **Function:** AI research, robotics development, tourism, human expansion
- **Governance:** International consortium operating on hybrid framework principles
- **Significance:** First true ocean-based city structure. First project the world builds and uses together.

### B. Desert Cities
- **Locations:** Sahara, Gobi, American Southwest, Australian Outback
- **Water Source:** Pipelines from nearest major waterways
- **Purification:** Desalination and purification en route
- **Housing:** Underground structures with large retractable fiberglass coverings providing full room-sized openings for natural light and outdoor air
- **Wildlife:** Integrated preserves, migration corridors, seasonal food production zones

### C. Mountain and Hillside Development
- **Construction:** Built into mountainsides, hills, and plateaus
- **Surface Impact:** Minimal — surface land returned to wildlife for centuries-long replenishment
- **Openings:** Large fiberglass retractable covers for full outdoor access
- **Climate:** Earth-insulated passive design with geothermal supplement

### D. Vertical Super-Cities
- **Structure:** Massive vertical buildings with fully integrated internal ecosystems
- **Environment:** Artificial light, air circulation, wind simulation, complete habitat
- **Nature Integration:** Retractable upper sections, internal green spaces, nature within the structure
- **Spacing:** More spacious than traditional housing — comfort, not confinement
- **Exits:** Multiple access points per residential unit

### E. Stacked Cities
- **Concept:** New city layers constructed above existing urban cores
- **Lower Level Conversion:** Restructured into wildlife habitats and natural preserves
- **Seasonal Use:** Food production zones active during specific times of year
- **Transit:** Vertical connection systems between layers

### F. Global Rail and Submerged Transit
- **Surface Rail:** High-speed rail connecting all countries
- **Submerged System:** Underwater tunnels connecting continents
- **Subway Reformation:** Existing subway systems converted and integrated into larger rail network
- **Impact:** Eliminates need for 100% of flight and private car travel for long-distance movement
- **Coastal Benefit:** Dramatically reduces need for large-scale new construction on East and West coasts

### G. Water and Hygiene
- **Ocean Purification:** Large-scale desalination plants on all continental coasts
- **Pipeline Networks:** Fresh water distribution from purification centers to inland regions
- **Public Bath Houses:** Stalls and open bays — priority deployment in East
- **Objective:** Universal clean water and hygiene access for all citizens
